01 — The System

A federated system of enterprise intelligence

At its core, the organisation is a network of distributed intelligence. Each employee operates a personal Shadow — a private system that understands how work is actually done.

These individual systems do not exist in isolation. They form a connected structure — where intelligence can scale without losing control.

The enterprise is no longer a collection of roles — it becomes a system of intelligence.

02 — Individual Intelligence

Intelligence begins with the individual

Each node represents an employee and their Shadow.

  • Private by design
  • Grounded in real workflows
  • Capable of assisting and executing work

AI starts where work actually happens.

03 — Structured Capability

Capability is structured at the organisational level

Departments define how intelligence becomes consistent. Through shared tools, workflows, and knowledge, individual systems align into structured capability.

From distributed intelligence to coordinated capability.

04 — Facia

Facia — the coordination and control plane

Every interaction is mediated through Facia. It governs:

  • How systems interact
  • How data moves
  • How actions are executed

Without Facia, intelligence fragments. With it, the system operates.

05 — CAO Operations

Operational control at the enterprise level

The Chief Agentic Officer operates as the control interface — overseeing how intelligence moves, interacts, and executes through Facia.

  • Monitors coordination across agents, tools, and systems
  • Enforces policies at the governance layer
  • Manages exceptions and escalations

Governance defined centrally — enforced in real time.

06 — Synthesis

A system that can actually operate

  • Intelligence remains distributed
  • Capability is structured
  • Coordination is controlled
  • Governance is continuous

From fragmented tools to a unified operating system.
